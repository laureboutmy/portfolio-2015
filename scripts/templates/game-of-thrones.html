Product design

Game of Thrones

Enhancing the video experience

scroll

For this school project, our only constraints were to use JavaScript and 3 APIs. It was made in November 2013 and I was Product Designer as well as the lead Front-end Developer.

We built a video player designed to enhance the viewing experience with social interactions and additional contents, all in sync with the episode. We chose to focus on the Game of Thrones series for its well known virality, its rollercoaster of emotions and its numerous characters and places.

Concept Client and viewers both winning

There is two aspects we had to think about: enriching the experience for the viewer and allowing the company producing the show to get something out of it (obviously, data and visibility). For these reasons, we needed our user to get social.

Gamification to encourage the user to connect

To incite the user to connect and share his social data, we created a simple reward system : the user earns different badges whenever he does something social. This system is introduced before the airing of the episode, that way people are aware of the existence of the website before the show begins, they can get ready and excited.

Getting social but not spoiled

Our product makes no sense if we are keeping our user from getting surprised by the TV show (especially with one like Game of Thrones). We were very careful of that when implementing our features. The tweets that we are sourcing are all tagged with the time they were posted at (ex:  “#8min”), they appear bit by bit in sync with the progress of the episode.

Functionalities Adding content while keeping our user focused

We decided we would create two types of timeline : the tweets one, so that the user can react whenever he wants and know what other people are thinking about the show, and the cards one. That is where we keep all of the additional content. At first we were thinking of adding notifications on the screen, but we then thought it would be too intrusive.

The cards

They are multiple type of cards:

  • The ones that encourage the user to share: the reaction card (we ask the user how he feels about the moment and publish it on Facebook), the gif card (inciting the user to share on Tumblr)
  • The ones that incite the user to buy: the goods card (buy a product from the show), the music card (telling the user what song it is and where to but it)
  • The knowlegeable ones: the counter card (informing the user of a fun counting thing, height of a wall, number of deaths in one scene), the quotes one (interview from the actors, etc) and the did-you-know? ones.

The progress bar

The progress bar serves as a reminder to the cards. There is a mark for each one of them and it navigates to the moment and through the timeline if you click one..

The browser

We didn’t want for the user to leave the player whenever a new content is display on the timeline, that’s why we created a little browser, that will open whenever the user clicks one a card. The game of throne theme music starts (if it’s not a video), to keep you in the atmosphere of the show.
